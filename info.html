Information about Pokémon

Basic Info
In the world of Pokémon, animals (and some plants) are replaced by creatures called Pokémon. Pokémon live in the wild or alongside people. People can capture them and store them within pokéballs to carry them around and interact with them in any way they please. The most common use of Pokémon is to use them in battle against each other where two or more of them fight each other until one faints (this is not as inhumane as it sounds). A large portion of the Pokémon world revolves around battles.
Types
Pokémon are categorized based on what type they are. Some Pokémon have a sort of elemenal type such as fire, water, or electricity, while others have simpler types such as bug, flying, or fighting. Pokémon can have up to two different types (but most only have one) while the first is the primary type. A Pokémon's type will largely be based on its lifestyle and battling style. Stats
The strengths and weaknesses of different Pokémon are shown by different stats. Each has a different set of hit points, attack, defense, special attack, special defense, and speed. Hit points, or HP, is a measure of endurance to show how much damage a Pokémon can take before it faints. Attack is a measure of how strong this Pokémon will be in delivering physical attacks. Defense is a measure of how well a Pokémon can defend against a physical attack. Special attack is similar to attack, but it is in relation to special (non-physical) attacks. Special defense is similar to defense, but it is in relation to defending against special attacks. Speed is a measure of how fast a Pokémon is so that it can try to attack before its opponent in battle.
